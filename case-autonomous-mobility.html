Autonomous mobility: cutting labeling time 3× without losing accuracy

Case Study · Autonomous & Mobility · Representative engagement

This is a representative engagement that reflects the kind of work and results we deliver. Details are anonymized; swap in your real client story when ready.

The challenge

An autonomous-mobility team had a growing backlog of multi-sensor sequences — camera plus LiDAR — that needed 3D cuboids, lane segmentation, and consistent object tracking across long clips. Their existing vendor was slow and inconsistent between annotators, and the safety-critical nature of the data meant errors were expensive.

What we did

  • AI pre-labeling on AnnotateMe generated draft cuboids and masks, cutting hands-on drawing time dramatically on routine frames.
  • Object tracking with interpolation propagated boxes across frames, so annotators set keyframes instead of labeling every frame.
  • A three-stage review workflow (annotate → validate → accept) enforced a consistent QA bar with sign-off at each stage.
  • Active-learning routing surfaced the lowest-confidence frames to senior reviewers first.

The results

3×
Faster throughput
6→2
Weeks of backlog cleared
99%+
Accepted-frame QA rate

Just as important as the speed: consistency improved, because every annotator corrected against the same AI baseline and passed through the same review gates. The team got training-ready data faster and could trust it.
